elements of dystopia

By: Zach McKenna

January 28, 2019


      Cellphones, while only becoming popular and mainstream in the last two decades, have warped society and driven individuals into a life of solitude and lack of conversational capability. This issue didnâ€™t fully prevent itself until the conception of the first mainstream smartphone in 2007, the iPhone 2G. This new piece of hardware allowed people to browse the web, text and call, and listen to music all in one simple package. This allowed people to escape reality like never before. At one point in time, people had to talk with other while at the gym or on public transport, but now people could put in music or wath videos and movies to suspend this vital human interaction. In schools, phones allowed for students to play games or listen to music while their teachers are giving lessons causing students to lose interest and stop listening all together. Kids in this generation are growing up without having to learn as many vital conversation skills that will be necessary in jobs and almost everything else in their life.

      Phones on our bodies at all times also pose the threat of being monitored by the companies selling them and even the government. Since our phones have microphone, GPS, and camera modules, they have the capability of keeping tabs on citizens at all times. With the growing viability of artificial intelligence and machine learning it would be relitively east to set up computers that sift through audio and camera footage looking for patterns and notifieng the government or compiling a profile based on habits. If the wrong company decided to partner with the US government or even a foreign government, there could be someone monitoring us at any time. With these devices becoming irreplacible parts of our everydayt life, nothing we do could be personal, there could be someone on the other side at any point in time. Even hackers could use this technology to monitor potential targets. These cellular devices have become to much of a part of our lives that we need to destroy the hold they have over us and keep us from being monitored everywhere we go.
